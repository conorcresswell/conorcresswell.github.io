The Hungry Bunch

Bring to the table win-win survival strategies to ensure proactive domination. At the end of the day, going forward, a new normal that has evolved from generation X is on the runway heading towards a streamlined cloud solution. User generated content in real-time will have multiple touchpoints for offshoring.

Client Name: Larry
Project Date: July 2020
Category: Brand Identity

Indentifying the Problem

Larry and Louise are the co-owners of a small mobile burger van based in the Winchester City Centre. They rely completely on physical customers that come to the van each day as the source of income for the business. They don’t have a brand identity besides their own names.


The Solution

The main goal of this project was to give Larry and Louise a strong visual identity that could be expanded across multiple online platforms. Making them recognisable and stand our amongst the larger fast-food chains that can populate apps like Deliveroo and JustEat. As well as digital assets, some of these would be transfered to physical products like packaging and menus

BARK APPLICATION

Apparel | Illustration

For my final major project, I undertook the project of designing, branding and building a proof-of-concept application. Emphasising on the user experience process and ensuring the product would be of practical use if it was to be released. The application in question was aimed at the audience of new pet owners, specifically dogs, and creating a solution that could be used as an online resource for these users.

The Mammoth Application

User Experience Design | Brand Identity

The brief for this project came from the Royal Society of Arts’ Student Design Awards 2017 brief number 11 called “Learning for Life”.

After reading the brief and taking notes I decided on the target audience of individuals between the ages of 55 and 66. The premise of the project is to design a solution that would encourage, empower and promote self-directed learning.
