Who Am I?

I love what I do and I hope you do too...

A little piece about the man behind the website.

Hello, my name is Conor and I’m a Illustrator and Graphic Designer based in the Southampton and Portsmouth area. I studied both my undergraduate and postgraduate degrees at the University of Winchester. Here is where I refined my illustration skills and expanded them to work in Print, Graphic Design, Branding and Website Design. I’m extremely passionate about what I do, spending most of my spare time looking at new design trends, filling my sketchbook and constantly trying to expand my design knowledge whilst occasionally playing video games.

The Bear and The Wolf

Two shirts for the price of one? Sold.

The Brief

The project began when I found a step-by-step guide to creating vector illustrations using Photoshop and Illustrator together. I really liked the attention to detail the illustration had and I wanted to incorporate this into one of my own drawings.

Research

After reading through the guide, I began to design my own illustration on paper. To do this, I used Pinterest to research different vector illustrations. I use Pinterest for a lot of different project so it was my first place for research.

The Process

After researching, I began to sketch a wolf, a bear and other native american animals. In the guide, they suggested to draw one half then using Photoshop to make the photo symmetrical. This would make it easier to draw in Illustrator.

Continuing on, some of the sketches focused on a specific section. This was as I like to draw different things for a project, then accumulate them into one illustration digitally.

The Development

After taking some of the sketches into Photoshop, I exported them as one image. This image was then used to draw over using the pen tool in Illustrator. After drawing one side of the bear, I duplicated one side of the face, flipped it then fused them together to look like one face.

After getting feedback from some of my peers in the design studio I was working in. I was told that it didn't look much like a bear at this point so I went onto Google to look at real bears. I then altered the illustration to make it more realistic. After this, I developed a colour scheme that would be used and painted this underneath the illustration on a separate layer in Illustrator.

After completing the main face, I went back to the sketches of the spears then went through a similar development process in creating it. Doing the outlines first and making sure it was symmetrical. Some of the staff sections of the spears I intentionally made asymmetrical as wood isn't naturally symmetrical.

The Outcome

After finalising the design both with and without colour. I found a Photoshop Smart Object mock up that I could use to show the design on a t-shirt. Adding feathers underneath the head of the spear to add to the native american influence on the illustration.

I was also given the opportunity to have this illustration printed onto a canvas bag for out End of Year Show. I took advantage of this to further understand how the illustration would look on different products. Overall I am pleased with the outcome of this project. Pushing myself to do something that I have not done before with any of my illustrations. I will continue to design different t-shirt designs with the hope of selling them commercially online through a third-party service.
