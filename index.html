Echelon Birds and Animals Project

*Sqwark*

  • Date: October to December 2015
  • Client: Echelon Networks
  • Category: Vector Illustration

The Brief

In this brief, the client wanted 84 different illustrations of animals and birds. They would be created in use for a game Echelon would be developing. The target audience of the game is children between the ages of 3 and 6 using a style that would be simple yet detailed enough to distinguish each animal.

I sketched out each bird and animal into my sketchbook. This is so I could ensure the consistency in the style.

After refining the outlines, I photographed the sketches and refined the lines using the pen tool in Adobe Illustrator. After this, I coloured them in using a gradient to define each section.

Mammoth Application

Not everyone over the age of 55 is a technophobe.

  • Date: September to December 2016
  • Client: None
  • Category: Brand Identity

The Brief

The brief for this project came from the Royal Society of Arts’ Student Design Awards brief number 11 called “Learning for Life”.

After reading the brief and taking notes I decided on the target audience of individuals between the ages of 55 and 66. This was an area I was made aware of within the brief itself. The basic premise of the project is to design a solution that would encourage, empower and promote self-directed learning outside of conventional places such as work or school.
